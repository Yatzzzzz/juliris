## Missing pages

From the pages I could verify, Juliris appears to be missing several high-value pages that the benchmark brands implicitly support through trust, education, and merchandising systems.[^8][^1][^2][^6][^7][^3]
These are the pages I would add first, in this order:

- Materials page: explain 316L stainless steel, why it is good for sensitive skin, what “hypoallergenic” means, and what Juliris avoids.
- Sensitive-skin FAQ: answer “Will this irritate me?”, “Can I shower with it?”, “Will it tarnish?”, “Is it nickel-free?”, “How do I care for it?”.
- Shipping and returns page: reduce hesitation before checkout.
- Jewelry care page: increases trust and post-purchase satisfaction.
- Best sellers page: your strongest conversion page for paid traffic and social links.
- Gift guide page: birthday, girlfriend, wife, minimalist, daily wear, under ₪100 / ₪200 / ₪300.
- Reviews / customer stories page: especially important because 95% of your revenue currently comes from fairs, so online shoppers need offline-proof translated into digital proof.
- “Seen at the fair” page: photos from booths, customers wearing pieces, and short quotes to turn your strongest real-world sales channel into trust content.


## Operations plan

Juliris already sells stainless-steel jewelry to women in a defined demographic, so the right move is to turn that product truth into a repeatable brand operation rather than posting randomly.
Your brand should position itself as: stylish everyday jewelry for women who want elegant pieces that are safe for sensitive skin, easy to wear, and durable enough for real life.

### Phase 1: Foundation, weeks 1–2

- Finalize brand message: one homepage promise, one product-page proof stack, one sensitive-skin FAQ, one materials page.
- Build a visual system: white or soft neutral background, consistent close-ups, skin shots, wear-in-life shots, and 3 overlay styles only, “Sensitive skin safe,” “316L steel,” “Everyday wear.”
- Set content pillars: education, styling, proof, founder story, offers.
- Install capture flows: email pop-up, abandoned cart, browse abandonment, wishlist reminder, back-in-stock.


### Phase 2: Content engine, weeks 3–6

- Instagram: 4–5 posts weekly, 2 reels, 1 carousel education post, 1 testimonial/proof post, 1 styling post, 1 product-drop or bundle post.
- TikTok: 3 short videos weekly, hooks like “Jewelry for sensitive skin,” “What I wear all day,” “Why cheap jewelry turns green.”
- Stories: daily, behind the scenes, packing orders, polls, “this or that,” new arrivals, booth clips, customer reposts.
- Pinterest: 10–15 pins weekly, product pins, gift guides, sensitive-skin tips, mood boards, occasion styling.
- Email: 1 campaign weekly, rotating between best sellers, education, user proof, and launches.


### Phase 3: Sales operations, weeks 7–12

- Launch 3 permanent bundles: “Daily essentials,” “Sensitive-skin starter set,” “Gift-ready set.”
- Run one monthly drop with a clear concept, not random uploads.
- Turn every fair into a content day: booth video, customer reactions, best sellers, FAQ clips, and post-event email follow-up.
- Ask every buyer for one of three assets: review text, selfie, or short video.
- Repost customer proof every week so trust compounds over time.


### Team workflow

- Monday: plan week, choose 5 products, write hooks and captions.
- Tuesday: shoot all product and lifestyle content in one batch.
- Wednesday: edit reels, carousels, stories, and email assets.
- Thursday: schedule posts and upload site banners.
- Friday/Saturday: capture live content from fairs or customer interactions.
- Sunday: review KPIs, save winners, repeat the best format.


### KPI dashboard

Track these every week:

- Product-page conversion rate.
- Add-to-cart rate by traffic source.
- Best-performing reel hooks.
- Email signup rate.
- Wishlist saves.
- Review collection rate.
- Revenue per collection / bundle.
- Return visitor rate.
